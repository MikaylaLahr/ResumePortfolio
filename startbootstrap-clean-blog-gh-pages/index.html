About Me

Hello! My name is Mikayla Lahr and I am currently a Cornell University - Sibley School of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering graduate student studying mechanical engineering concentrating on robotics. I am a science-minded person who loves the challenge of solving technical problems.

After graduating with a Bachelor of Science in mechanical engineering from Cornell University in December 2022, I began working as a Systems Engineer with General Dynamics - Mission Systems. In this position I was involved with designing new technology for defense systems and spacecraft development. Following a year of working, I made the decision to pursue a Master of Engineering degree (expected graduation date of May 2025).

My Master of Engineering classes have included Fast Robots and Spacecraft Technology and Systems Architecture. I am currently member of the Collective Embodied Intelligence Lab under the direction of Cornell University Professor Kristin H. Petersen whose research involves design and coordination of large robot collectives (https://cei.ece.cornell.edu/). Furthermore, I worked on robotic research projects as a summer employee in Cornell University's College of Human Ecology - Human Centered Design.

During my undergraduate education, I was a member of Cornell Mars Rover Project Team which is made up of 70 members from a variety of majors at Cornell University. As the Cornell Mars Rover Drives Lead my group was responsible for designing, building, and testing the mechanical drive system of the rover, including suspension and wheels.

Additionaly, I was a research member of the Solar Heating and Thermoelectric generation group under the direction of Cornell Professor Zhiting Tian. My responsibilities included Computer-Aided Design (CAD) development, system thermodynamics and Ansys simulation modeling. A patent for this research and can be found at: https://patents.google.com/patent/WO2023158633A1/en?inventor=Mikayla+LAHR

In the summer of 2022, I had the opportunity to serve in a very rewarding mechanical engineering internship at CSArch, an architectural, engineering and construction management firm. I was able to explore several aspects of the company's work within the engineering, architectural and construction sectors. Through this internship I became very proficient in Autodesk Revit to create BIM (Building Information Modeling) models for current projects.

I am excited about the opportunity to make a difference and look for ways to tackle engineering challenges in a dynamic team environment. For any additional information or questions please feel free to contact me at mrl242@cornell.edu
