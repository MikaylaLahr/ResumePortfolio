TEG Systems Lead: Fall 2022

Research and Prototype

This multi-year research project integrates solar heating, thermal storage, and thermoelectric generation in a cookstove. It is hoped that this innovative technology will provide a solution to energy access and help mitigate climate change as well. This solar cookstove is comprised of an optical concentration mechanism, thermoelectric generation unit and thermal storage. My responsibilities in this group project include CAD design development and system thermodynamics. In addition, I developed a PLECS (software that models circuits) simulation of the co-generation system. PLECS simulates the heat transfer between the layers of the co-generation system during charging and discharging phases. I utilized MATLAB coding to determine the thermal resistance between each material layer. Thermal resistances, specific heat capacity, and incoming solar power solved for in the MATLAB code are used in the PLECS simulation.

A Google Patent was awarded for this research. (WO2023158633A1 - Combined solar heating and thermoelectric generation system - Google Patents) https://patents.google.com/patent/WO2023158633A1/en?inventor=Mikayla+LAHR
