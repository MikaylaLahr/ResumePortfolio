Internet of Things - Agricultural Project

Spring 2022

Internet of Things (IoT) Vineyard Project

My project team worked to bring community level engagement of the Internet of Things (IoT) to agriculture. Specifically, we worked with Sheldrake Point and Knapp Wineries, both located on Cayuga Lake in the New York State Finger Lakes region, to develop IoT systems that can best suit their needs. The team focused on the application of IoT using sensors to monitor elements essential for grape cultivation. Through communication with the wineries the list of parameters was narrowed down to temperature, leaf wetness, and soil moisture to effectively monitor the chances of frost occurring as well as disease. Both wineries were also interested in monitoring the temperature of the winery tanks, which was something that the team did not previously consider. I learned that most wineries have built in temperature monitoring for their tanks, so we only needed to integrate an IoT system with their existing system to send alerts should the temperature exit an acceptable range (as this could ruin a batch). According to the winemaker, this is a frequent issue even with modern temperature regulation systems so having an IoT alert system would be invaluable. The team explored different types of sensors that would best serve their needs and discussed a few sensor options with the winery to get their opinion on what would work best. For the winery, next steps include determining how to best integrate IoT with the existing temperature monitors and deciding how often temperature needs to be measured.

Community Engagement

The engineering designs were shaped by community engagement. A design project can only be successful with guidance and advice from community partners. The IoT system needed to provide a benefit to the community partners. Communication with the wineries helped determine the best way to plan out an IoT system that would be a reasonably priced and still help the monitor conditions in the vineyard and winery. Cost was a concern for the wineries which was an important factor in the IoT design project.

I learned a great deal on how to conduct an interview and ask unbiased questions. Team members and I learned about their experiences and perspectives through email as well as scheduling meetings on Zoom. When working with community engagement it is important to remember the three components: relationship, process, and results.
