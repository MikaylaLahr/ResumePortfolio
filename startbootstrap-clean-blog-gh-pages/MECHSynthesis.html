Slider Dividers - Mechanical Synthesis

Spring 2021

Project Requirements

The Divider Slider was created for a Mechanical Synthesis project to design something that would be useful to essential COVID workers. I was responsible for modeling the final project in Fusion 360 which involved the curves of the divider fabric and frame. I was pleased how the CAD model represents a real-life product. A challenging part of the project was creating a design that would reduce the space in between each divider. After receiving feedback from the OPD Preliminary Design Review on reducing the space in between each divider, I thought through several ways to solve this issue. I decided that creating a clip-in panel, as shown in the CAD Model, would be the most effective option for the user while also preserving the functionality of the design.

User Needs

The project involved a user needs interview of essential workers from the Albany University COVID Vaccination Site. Before the interview, my partner and myself came up with a procedure to mitigate the effect of confirmation and social desirability bias was followed. As a team we agreed that all information would be recorded and evaluated, none would be ignored. Interview strategies and question sets were used to encourage greater insight through personal stories and obtain relevant unbiased information. The interview plan contained questions as well as a script which included topics such as “Please take us through a typical day at your work as an essential worker” and based on their response ask them about pain points; ask about safety and the work environment; follow up with more specific questions based on their responses. An issue that the essential workers brought up was how loud the vaccine area can be with dozens of people surrounding the booths and tents where background noise can be heard through canvas walls making it difficult for assistants, nurses, and hard-of-hearing patients to communicate. We concluded that the need to constantly speak louder than the sound level of normal conversation caused quite a lot of strain during a work shift and was an important concern of essential workers. Therefore, reducing the noise level at the vaccine center through a station design modification would help communication and the work environment, was an important issue to address.

Design Process

Another interesting and valuable part of the project was completing the Crazy-8s idea generation exercise and designing Storyboards. The Crazy-8s exercise allowed me to brainstorm ideas that I may not have thought of, examine each idea, and identify which ideas were feasible and beneficial. The Storyboard exercise for a chosen design concept was beneficial because it allowed us to narrow our focus towards a particular product, while remaining creative in the design process. The Storyboard helped to visualize the essential worker’s experience and lead to finding the best design concept.

The engineering design process for creating a unique design for a divider was a very challenging part of the project. My partner and I went through several different design ideas for what would be the most effective way to create a collapsible divider that is functional and solves the issue of reducing noise at the site. We wanted to create a divider that was also able to reduce in height if certain sections of the site or groups of essential workers decided that it would be beneficial to be able to communicate effectively between each area. In addition, we also wanted to reduce the space that each divider would take up during transportation to the site. The dividers’ ability to reduce in height allows for less space needed for transportation. A challenging part of the design process was determining the most effective way to reduce the size of the dividers. Ultimately, it was decided that thick noise reducing fabric would be used as the divider material. The fabric would be connected to rods on both sides (using clips on the top plate) and would be able to fold down to reduce the height of the divider. The fabric would also be able to be removed from the clips if the user wanted to clean the dividers.
