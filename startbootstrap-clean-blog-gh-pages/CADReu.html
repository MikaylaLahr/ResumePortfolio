Spring 2021

Reuleaux Kinematic Model

The Reuleaux kinematic “the study of geometry of motion” model I choose for my Mechanical Synthesis class project was the Cartwright Straight Line Mechanism designed by Franz Reuleaux (1829-1905) (Moon & Ford). The Cartwright Straight Line mechanism is part of Reuleaux S-series that “change circular motion into straight-line motion” (Kempe, 1877, as cited in Moon & Ford). The handle located on the back of the Cartwright caused the twin gears to rotate the twin gears causing a common slider crank attached to both gears to move a vertical center bar up and down in a straight line. My Cartwright mechanism design is set on a wooden platform and made from cast iron and brass parts. The greatest challenge was designing the twin gears and then connecting them to the slider crank for the mechanism to move the vertical bar in a straight-line. This mechanism appealed to me because it was challenging and the design features remarkable.

Kitchen Teapot - CAD

The simple task of creating a teapot becomes quite difficult when you attempt to replicate the task using CAD (Fusion 360). I choose this kitchen item for my first Mechanical Synthesis class project. At first, the teapot design seemed straightforward but as I began to explore all the components required for assembly it became clear how challenging this task would be to complete.
